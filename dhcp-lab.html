DHCP Lab

Overview

This lab demonstrates the configuration and implementation of Dynamic Host Configuration Protocol (DHCP) across three subnets with distinct setups. DHCP automates IP address assignment, reducing manual configuration efforts. The lab configures one subnet with direct DHCP from a server, another using a router as a relay agent, and a third with a router acting as a DHCP server.

What is DHCP? DHCP assigns IP addresses, subnet masks, gateways, and other network parameters to devices dynamically. This lab explores server-based DHCP, relay agents with the `ip helper-address` command, and router-integrated DHCP services, ensuring efficient IP management across VLANs.

The topology includes three LANs: LAN1 (192.168.10.0/24) with PCs and a DHCP server, LAN2 (192.168.20.0/24) using a relay agent, and LAN3 (192.168.30.0/24) with a router-based DHCP server. OSPF enables routing, and the lab verifies DHCP lease assignments and inter-subnet connectivity.

Topology

  • Devices:
    • Router0, Router1 - Cisco 2911.
    • Switch0, Switch1, Switch2 - Cisco 2950.
    • Server0 - DHCP server (connected to Switch0).
    • PC0, PC1 (connected to Switch0), PC2, PC3 (connected to Switch1), PC4, PC5 (connected to Switch2).
  • Connections:
    • Router0 G0/0 to Switch0 F0/1 (LAN1).
    • Router0 G0/1 to Switch1 F0/1 (LAN2).
    • Router0 G0/2 to Router1 G0/2 (point-to-point).
    • Router1 G0/0 to Switch2 F0/1 (LAN3).
    • Switch0 F0/2 to Server0, F0/3 to PC0, F0/4 to PC1.
    • Switch1 F0/2 to PC2, F0/3 to PC3.
    • Switch2 F0/2 to PC4, F0/3 to PC5.
  • IP Addressing:
    • LAN1: 192.168.10.0/24 (Router0 G0/0: 192.168.10.1, Server0: 192.168.10.2, PC0: DHCP, PC1: DHCP).
    • LAN2: 192.168.20.0/24 (Router0 G0/1: 192.168.20.1, PC2: DHCP, PC3: DHCP).
    • LAN3: 192.168.30.0/24 (Router1 G0/0: 192.168.30.1, PC4: DHCP, PC5: DHCP).
    • Point-to-Point: 192.168.40.0/30 (Router0 G0/2: 192.168.40.1, Router1 G0/2: 192.168.40.2).

Verification

OSPF neighbor tables confirm adjacencies between Router0 and Router1. DHCP bindings on Server0 show PC0 and PC1 leases from `serverpool` (192.168.10.100–199) and PC2 and PC3 from `pool1` (192.168.20.100–199). Router1 DHCP bindings show PC4 and PC5 leases from `LAN3_POOL` (192.168.30.100–199).

Connectivity Test

A successful ping from PC0 (e.g., 192.168.10.100) to PC2 (e.g., 192.168.20.100) verifies inter-subnet routing via OSPF. PC0 and PC1 receive IPs from Server0, PC2 and PC3 from Server0 via Router0 relay, and PC4 and PC5 from Router1, confirming DHCP functionality across all setups.
