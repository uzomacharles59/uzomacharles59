RIP Lab

Overview

This lab demonstrates the configuration and operation of the Routing Information Protocol version 2 (RIPv2), a distance-vector routing protocol, in a multi-router network. RIPv2 uses hop count as its metric and is ideal for small to medium-sized networks. The lab features three routers connected via serial links, each with a LAN and a loopback interface, showcasing dynamic routing and convergence.

What is RIP? RIP is a simple protocol that advertises routes every 30 seconds, with a maximum hop count of 15. RIPv2 improves upon RIPv1 by supporting VLSM and including subnet mask information in updates. Key features include automatic summarization (disabled here with `no auto-summary`) and multicast updates (224.0.0.9).

The lab verifies RIP’s ability to learn and propagate routes across the network, ensuring connectivity between all devices and loopback interfaces.

Topology

  • Devices:
    • Router0 (HQ), Router1 (Branch 1), Router2 (Branch 2) - Cisco 2911.
    • Switch0 (HQ LAN), Switch1 (Branch 1 LAN), Switch2 (Branch 2 LAN) - Cisco 2950.
    • PC0 (HQ), PC1 (Branch 1), PC2 (Branch 2).
  • Connections:
    • Router0 G0/0 to Switch0 F0/1 (HQ LAN).
    • Router1 G0/0 to Switch1 F0/1 (Branch 1 LAN).
    • Router2 G0/0 to Switch2 F0/1 (Branch 2 LAN).
    • Router0 S0/0/0 to Router1 S0/0/0 (serial).
    • Router1 S0/0/1 to Router2 S0/0/1 (serial).
    • Router2 S0/0/0 to Router0 S0/0/1 (serial).
    • Switch0 F0/2 to PC0, Switch1 F0/2 to PC1, Switch2 F0/2 to PC2.
  • VLANs: VLAN 10 (HQ), VLAN 20 (Branch 1), VLAN 30 (Branch 2).
  • IP Addressing:
    • VLAN 10: 192.168.1.0/24 (PC0: 192.168.1.2, Router0 G0/0: 192.168.1.254).
    • VLAN 20: 192.168.2.0/24 (PC1: 192.168.2.2, Router1 G0/0: 192.168.2.254).
    • VLAN 30: 192.168.3.0/24 (PC2: 192.168.3.2, Router2 G0/0: 192.168.3.254).
    • Router0-Router1: 192.168.12.0/30 (R0: 192.168.12.1, R1: 192.168.12.2).
    • Router1-Router2: 192.168.23.0/30 (R1: 192.168.23.1, R2: 192.168.23.2).
    • Router2-Router0: 192.168.13.0/30 (R2: 192.168.13.1, R0: 192.168.13.2).
    • Loopback0: R0: 192.168.10.1/32, R1: 192.168.20.1/32, R2: 192.168.30.1/32.

Configurations

Router0 (HQ)

router rip
 version 2
 network 192.168.1.0
 network 192.168.12.0
 network 192.168.13.0
 network 192.168.10.0
 no auto-summary
exit

RIP Verification

The routing table on Router0 shows all RIP-learned routes (marked with "R"), including networks from Router1 and Router2. The `show ip protocols` command confirms RIPv2 is active with the specified networks advertised.

Connectivity Test

A successful ping from PC0 (192.168.1.2) to PC1 (192.168.2.2), PC0 (192.168.1.2) to PC2 (192.168.3.2) and Router1 Loopback0 (192.168.20.1) verifies that RIP is routing traffic correctly across the network.
