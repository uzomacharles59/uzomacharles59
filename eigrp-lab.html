EIGRP Lab

Overview

This lab demonstrates the configuration and operation of Enhanced Interior Gateway Routing Protocol (EIGRP), an advanced distance-vector routing protocol developed by Cisco. EIGRP uses the Diffusing Update Algorithm (DUAL) for loop-free routing and supports features like fast convergence, unequal-cost load balancing, and VLSM. The lab features four routers in a ring topology, each with a LAN and a loopback interface, showcasing EIGRP’s neighbor relationships and route selection based on composite metrics (bandwidth and delay).

What is EIGRP? EIGRP advertises routes using a reliable update mechanism, maintains a topology table, and selects the best path based on a composite metric. It operates within an autonomous system (AS) and supports both IPv4 and IPv6. Key features include automatic summarization (disabled here with `no auto-summary`) and the ability to handle complex topologies.

The lab verifies EIGRP’s ability to establish neighbor relationships, propagate routes, and ensure connectivity across the network.

Topology

  • Devices:
    • Router0 (HQ), Router1 (Branch 1), Router2 (Branch 2), Router3 (Remote) - Cisco 2911.
    • Switch0 (HQ LAN), Switch1 (Branch 1 LAN), Switch2 (Branch 2 LAN), Switch3 (Remote LAN) - Cisco 2950.
    • PC0 (HQ), PC1 (Branch 1), PC2 (Branch 2), PC3 (Remote).
  • Connections:
    • Router0 G0/0 to Switch0 F0/1 (HQ LAN).
    • Router1 G0/0 to Switch1 F0/1 (Branch 1 LAN).
    • Router2 G0/0 to Switch2 F0/1 (Branch 2 LAN).
    • Router3 G0/0 to Switch3 F0/1 (Remote LAN).
    • Router0 S0/0/0 to Router1 S0/0/0 (serial).
    • Router1 S0/0/1 to Router2 S0/0/0 (serial).
    • Router2 S0/0/1 to Router3 S0/0/0 (serial).
    • Router3 S0/0/1 to Router0 S0/0/1 (serial).
    • Switch0 F0/2 to PC0, Switch1 F0/2 to PC1, Switch2 F0/2 to PC2, Switch3 F0/2 to PC3.
  • VLANs: VLAN 10 (HQ), VLAN 20 (Branch 1), VLAN 30 (Branch 2), VLAN 40 (Remote).
  • IP Addressing:
    • VLAN 10: 192.168.1.0/24 (PC0: 192.168.1.2, Router0 G0/0: 192.168.1.254).
    • VLAN 20: 192.168.2.0/24 (PC1: 192.168.2.2, Router1 G0/0: 192.168.2.254).
    • VLAN 30: 192.168.3.0/24 (PC2: 192.168.3.2, Router2 G0/0: 192.168.3.254).
    • VLAN 40: 192.168.4.0/24 (PC3: 192.168.4.2, Router3 G0/0: 192.168.4.254).
    • Router0-Router1: 192.168.12.0/30 (R0: 192.168.12.1, R1: 192.168.12.2).
    • Router1-Router2: 192.168.23.0/30 (R1: 192.168.23.1, R2: 192.168.23.2).
    • Router2-Router3: 192.168.34.0/30 (R2: 192.168.34.1, R3: 192.168.34.2).
    • Router3-Router0: 192.168.13.0/30 (R3: 192.168.13.1, R0: 192.168.13.2).
    • Loopback0: R0: 192.168.10.1/32, R1: 192.168.20.1/32, R2: 192.168.30.1/32, R3: 192.168.40.1/32.

EIGRP Verification

The EIGRP neighbor table on Router0 shows active neighbors (Router1 and Router3), confirming successful adjacency. The routing table displays EIGRP-learned routes (marked with "D"), including all LANs, serial links, and loopbacks.

Connectivity Test

A successful ping from PC0 (192.168.1.2) to PC3 (192.168.4.2) and Router3 Loopback0 (192.168.40.1) verifies that EIGRP is routing traffic correctly across the network.
